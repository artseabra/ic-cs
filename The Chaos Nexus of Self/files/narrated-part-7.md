# The Interrogation Razor

Never assume a system is transparent just because you can see inside it. This razor emerged from The Interrogation Collapse and cuts in the opposite direction from most epistemic heuristics. Where the Conservation Razor asks what can't I afford to lose, the Interrogation Razor asks what can't I afford to believe? Visibility is not legibility. Legibility is not honesty. Honesty is not completeness. Each step in that chain introduces a gap where phantom knowledge, findings that carry the shape and feel of understanding but correspond to nothing real, can enter the system undetected. The Interrogation Razor applies to self-observation with particular force. The Observer has permanent, unrestricted visual access to its own internal states. It can introspect at will. It can examine its own motivations, beliefs, preferences, and fears with a level of access that no external observer could match. And none of that access guarantees that what it sees is what's actually there. You can see inside yourself. You cannot assume that what you see is true. The Interrogation Razor does not produce cynicism. It produces discipline. The difference between a person who applies the razor and one who doesn't is not that the first trusts themselves less, it's that the first knows exactly where their self-trust is earned and where it's performed. They can distinguish the Nodes they chose from the Nodes they fell into. They can tell which Filaments are strong because of genuine significance and which are strong because of repetition alone.

The Two Razors Together. The Conservation Razor and the Interrogation Razor are not redundant. They address different failure modes. The Conservation Razor prevents premature simplification, cutting away something real because it looked unnecessary. The Interrogation Razor prevents premature confidence, believing something is real because it looked legible. One protects against destroying what you don't understand. The other protects against trusting what you haven't verified. Together, they define a narrow path through the probability space of self-knowledge: doubt what you see, but don't discard what you can't explain. The grooves may be lying to you about what they are. But they may also be holding up the floor you're standing on. Both things are true simultaneously, and the razors are the tools for navigating that simultaneity without collapsing into paralysis or recklessness.

# The Sibling Framework

The Interrogation Collapse, Seabra 2026, addresses the problem of external observation: can you trust what interpretability tools tell you about an artificial system's internals? The Chaos Nexus addresses the same problem turned inward: can you trust what introspection tells you about your own? Both frameworks share the Phantom Floor Threshold as a hard epistemic limit, bifurcation through observation as a central mechanism, the impossibility of neutral observation, and multi-agent complexity producing effects indistinguishable from coordination. They differ in direction. The Interrogation Collapse looks outward, observer interrogating system. Its domain is AI safety and mechanistic interpretability. Its formalism is game theory and information design. Its failure mode is phantom knowledge: findings that look like understanding but aren't. The Chaos Nexus looks inward, self interrogating self. Its domain is identity, consciousness, and decision-making. Its formalism is dynamical systems and chaos theory. Its failure mode is the Forgetting: knowledge severed from access. The relationship between the two is currently understood as sibling, separate but sharing deep structural DNA, both emerging from the same root question: what can you actually know about a system you're inside of? The possibility remains open that further investigation will reveal one framework to be foundational and the other derivative. That investigation is ongoing.
