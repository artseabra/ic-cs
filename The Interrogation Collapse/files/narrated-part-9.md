# Origin Note

The Interrogation Collapse emerged from a first-principles exploration of the prisoner's dilemma, initiated by the author while reading Algorithms to Live By by Brian Christian and Tom Griffiths. The initial thought experiment — introducing a game-theory-literate specialist into a multi-agent interrogation scenario with variable information regimes — was recognized as an independent reconstruction of mechanism design. The framework evolved through application of this game-theoretic structure to the problem of mechanistic interpretability in AI safety, yielding the concepts formalized in this paper. The framework shares philosophical DNA with two related bodies of the author's work: The Chaos Nexus of Self, a philosophical framework treating identity as a probabilistic system shaped by decisions across branching realities, and the Conservation Razor, a decision heuristic stating do not choose the simplest explanation — choose the one that removes only what reality can afford to lose. These connections are acknowledged and formally bridged in Section Five of this paper. They constitute directions for subsequent work.
