## The Papers

### [The Interrogation Collapse](./the-interrogation-collapse/the-interrogation-collapse.md)
*A Framework for Evaluating Epistemic Validity in Systems Transparency*

The field of mechanistic interpretability (MI) operates under an implicit assumption: that the systems being observed are passive subjects of investigation. This paper challenges that assumption by reframing interpretability as a **strategic interaction** between observer and system — an interrogation in which the outcome depends not only on the tools employed but on the information regime governing who knows what about whom.

**Core contribution:** Eight formal concepts including the Phantom Floor Threshold (PFT), Falsifiability Decay, Observation Decoupling, and a four-tier Collapse Classification (Structural Misparse → Confident Phantom → Performed Compliance → Coordinated Opacity).

**Keywords:** mechanistic interpretability, AI safety, epistemic game theory, deceptive alignment, Bayesian persuasion, adversarial epistemology

### [The Chaos Nexus of Self](./the-chaos-nexus-of-self/)
*Identity as Strange Attractor in Probabilistic Phase Space*

A framework for understanding human identity as a strange attractor operating within a high-dimensional probability space shaped by accumulated choices. Treats conscious self-observation as a stochastic perturbation capable of dislodging the self from one attractor basin into another.

**Core contribution:** Seven formal concepts (Observer, Exposure, Filament, Payload, Nodes, Substrate, Archive) derived from a single natural analogy — the squirrel burying nuts in a forest — and formalized into a complete technical architecture of identity dynamics.

**Keywords:** identity, strange attractor, chaos theory, dynamical systems, consciousness, probability space, epistemic limits

## Shared Architecture

Both frameworks emerge from the same root question:

> *What can you actually know about a system you're inside of?*

IC looks outward — observer interrogating an external system, formalized through game theory and information design.

CS turns the lens inward — self interrogating self, formalized through dynamical systems and chaos theory.

Both share the **Phantom Floor Threshold** as a hard epistemic limit. Both treat observation as participatory rather than passive. Both conclude that the act of looking changes what is found.

**Shared heuristics:**
- **Conservation Razor** — Do not choose the simplest explanation; choose the one that removes only what reality can afford to lose.
- **Interrogation Razor** — Never assume a system is transparent just because you can see inside it.
